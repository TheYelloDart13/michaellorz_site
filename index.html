How's Your Head?
How's Your Heart?

It's my favorite way to check in with people — and a pretty good introduction to my approach to both life and acting. I'm someone who is serious about my craft, not about myself. Growing up an only child gives you a lot of time to make funny faces and voices at yourself in the bathroom mirror, and while NYU theater training was nice and all — I credit my ability to transform into no fewer than 525,600 different characters to those days of being bored after brushing my teeth. Actor, comedian, Twitch streamer, and Midwestern choir kid at heart, I'm Breitbart's worst nightmare, using They/Them and He/Him pronouns indiscriminately. I'm interested in exploring art that deepens empathy, makes bold statements, and enjoys a bit of camp.

What I've Done

Feel free to click that little IMDB link above, but you can see my television work on Gotham (FOX), Difficult People (HULU), and Elementary (CBS). In the world of video games, recent releases include the smash indie hit, Date Everything (creators Robbie Daymond, Ray Chase, & Max Mittelman) where I played a sassy lamp... There are three AAA video game titles under way that I'm dying to tell the world more about (when I'm allowed to). I'm expecting my first role in an animated feature to be out at the end of 2026, and on the dubbing side of things you can hear my voice all over the various streaming services, but most notably I was the American voice of Koji in Netflix's My Happy Marriage and Hiroto The Paradox in Hulu's Ishura. I've also had the privilege of voicing multiple characters in the narrative podcasts: Academy (Wondery), Very Unbecoming (Audible), The Last City (Wondery), and Who Killed Avril Lavigne (Super Normal Media) — as well as several voices in the award-winning immersive experience, The Ladder (Hatch Escapes - LA).

What We Could Do Together

If you're on the hunt for a quick-witted performer with an alarming number of characters living inside his larynx, I'm the sprightly, gender-queer actor you've been searching for. Having started my career doing Musical Theater in NYC, I'm more than prepared to take on your singing roles and do lackluster jazz squares in your sound booth. Now based on the west coast, you can catch me climbing the ladder of improv training at The Groundlings School, should you need a comedic improviser on your project. I've also got a decade of experience bringing a vast (and bizarre) spectrum of characters to life through motion capture — from the Aflac Duck to omnipotent celestial beings. My God.. what have I NOT been doing...? Football. I've not been doing any football. Like, in any capacity. I feel like it's just too much starting and stoppi— I'm getting away from myself, now.
